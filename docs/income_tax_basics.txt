Income Tax Slabs for FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26) - Old Regime
Under the old tax regime, individual taxpayers below 60 years of age are taxed as follows: Income up to Rs 2.5 lakh is exempt from tax. Income from Rs 2.5 lakh to Rs 5 lakh is taxed at 5%. Income from Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh is taxed at 20%. Income above Rs 10 lakh is taxed at 30%. A rebate under Section 87A of up to Rs 12,500 is available if total income does not exceed Rs 5 lakh, effectively making income up to Rs 5 lakh tax-free.

Income Tax Slabs for FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26) - New Regime
The new tax regime is the default regime from FY 2023-24 onwards. The slabs are: Income up to Rs 3 lakh is exempt. Income from Rs 3 lakh to Rs 7 lakh is taxed at 5%. Income from Rs 7 lakh to Rs 10 lakh is taxed at 10%. Income from Rs 10 lakh to Rs 12 lakh is taxed at 15%. Income from Rs 12 lakh to Rs 15 lakh is taxed at 20%. Income above Rs 15 lakh is taxed at 30%. Under the new regime, a rebate under Section 87A is available for income up to Rs 7 lakh, making income up to Rs 7 lakh effectively tax-free. However, most deductions like 80C, 80D, and HRA exemption are NOT available under the new regime.

Senior Citizen Tax Slabs - Old Regime
Senior citizens aged 60 to 80 years get a higher basic exemption limit of Rs 3 lakh under the old regime. Super senior citizens aged 80 years and above get a basic exemption limit of Rs 5 lakh under the old regime. Under the new regime, the slabs are the same for all age groups regardless of senior citizen status.

PAN Card Requirements
Every person whose total income exceeds the basic exemption limit must have a Permanent Account Number (PAN). PAN is a 10-digit alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department. PAN is mandatory for filing income tax returns, opening bank accounts with deposits exceeding Rs 50,000, purchasing or selling immovable property valued above Rs 10 lakh, and for securities transactions. Aadhaar can be used as a substitute for PAN in certain cases after linking.

Income Tax Return Filing Deadlines
The due date for filing income tax returns for salaried individuals and persons not requiring audit is July 31 of the assessment year. For businesses requiring audit, the due date is October 31. For transfer pricing cases, the due date is November 30. A belated return can be filed by December 31 of the assessment year with a penalty. If total income exceeds Rs 5 lakh, the return must be filed electronically. If income is below Rs 5 lakh and the person is a super senior citizen (80+ years), the return can be filed in paper form.

Advance Tax Rules
Any person whose estimated tax liability for the year exceeds Rs 10,000 must pay advance tax. Advance tax is paid in 4 installments: 15% by June 15, 45% by September 15, 75% by December 15, and 100% by March 15. Failure to pay advance tax attracts interest under Section 234B (for shortfall) and Section 234C (for deferment). Senior citizens not having income from business or profession are exempt from paying advance tax.

Standard Deduction
A standard deduction of Rs 50,000 is available to all salaried employees and pensioners under both the old and new tax regimes. This was increased from Rs 40,000 to Rs 50,000 from FY 2019-20 onwards. The standard deduction is available under the new regime from FY 2023-24 onwards. No bills or proof of expenses are required to claim the standard deduction. It is automatically applied to salary income.
